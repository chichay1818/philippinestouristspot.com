Tourist Spots in Cordillera Administrative Region- Apayao


Apayao is a landlocked province in the Philippines' Luzon province's Cordillera Administrative Region (CAR). The economy of Apapao is mostly focused on agriculture, with rice and corn being the two main crops. The province is renowned for its rich cultural legacy, with indigenous groups maintaining customs and ceremonies.

Bayugao Falls

The three-layered Bayugao Falls are situated in Brgy. Turod is located in Luna, Apayao, a short distance from the Poblacion. A group of miniature waterfalls with naturally formed ponds is called Bayugao Falls. Their refreshing, glistening waters create a scene that accentuates the surrounding woods.

Long gone was the time that when you hear about Apayao province, Kalinga will be the first to pop into your mind. Apayao, once part of the sole province of Kalinga-Apayao (now defunct), is making a name for itself. The local government of Apayao has been active during the past few years in promoting and exploring its hidden gems. According to the local dialect of the Isneg tribe, "apayao" means "negotiable river". Branded as "Cordillera's Last Frontier of Nature's Richness," you'll discover that Apayao isn't all just rivers.

The cool clean water falls of Bayugao is a refreshing tourist attraction, around 4 km away from Poblacion. This is a 3-deck natural pools rising about 12 meters from each other mark. The waterfalls from the top pool cascade to the next in fluid succession. These natural pools provide clean, cool mountain spring water for everyone to enjoy swimming.

Nearby Accomodation

While there may not be hotels directly at Bayugao Falls, these options provide convenient access to Bayugao Falls and other attractions in the area, allowing travelers to experience the beauty of Cordillera Administrative Region's natural landscapes.


Tinglayan Town Proper

Tinglayan Town Proper - Tinglayan serves as the gateway to Bayugao Falls, and you may find basic accommodations such as guesthouses, inns, or homestays in the town proper. These accommodations may offer simple amenities and are often operated by locals.
