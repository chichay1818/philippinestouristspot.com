Tourist Spots in CAR Region



Tinongchol Burial Site

Located in the town of Hingyon, the Tinongchol Burial Site is an ancient burial cave that provides a unique glimpse into Ifugao burial practices. The site features wooden coffins stacked within the cave, showcasing the cultural significance of ancestral veneration and traditional burial customs.


Here, century-old mummies were kept in huge jars or makeshift coffins. The mummification practice by the ancestors made it possible for some cadavers to remain intact; some have their tattoos evident up to now.

It is almost as big as a regular three-storey building, houses centuries-old mummies. This came about through the labor of the people of the old times, who bored holes on the solid shoulders where they interred their dead.

Making the holes and spaces in the rock was not easy. No iron tools were found inside when the tomb was discovered. The Ibalois drenched the rock with a ginger solution that softened the stone and made digging easier.
