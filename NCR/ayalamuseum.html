Metro Manila

Metro Manila, the National Capital Region of the Philippines, is a bustling metropolis that serves as the country's political, economic, and cultural center. It is composed of 16 cities and one municipality, which together form a highly urbanized and dynamic region.

The area was established in 1975 to consolidate the then-independent cities of Manila, Quezon City, Caloocan, Las Piñas, Makati, Malabon, Mandaluyong, Marikina, Muntinlua, Navotas, Parañaque, Pasay, Pasig, San Juan, Taguig, and Valenzuela, along with Pateros municipality. This move aimed to streamline the administration and development of the region, which is crucial for the nation's progress.



Ayala Museum

The Ayala Museum is a private museum located in Makati City, within the National Capital Region of the Philippines. Established in 1967, the museum is owned and operated by the Ayala Foundation, a non-profit organization affiliated with the Ayala Corporation, one of the country's largest conglomerates.
The Ayala Museum aims to promote a deeper understanding and appreciation of Philippine history, culture, and art. It houses an extensive collection of artifacts, artworks, and other materials that showcase the rich heritage of the Philippines. The museum's exhibits are divided into several categories, including archaeology, ethnography, and contemporary art.

Some notable exhibits at the Ayala Museum include:
"Spoliarium," a replica of the famous painting by Filipino artist Juan Luna, which won a gold medal at the 1884 Paris Exposition. The original painting is housed in the National Museum of Finland.
"The Baybayin Gallery," which showcases the pre-Hispanic script used by various indigenous groups in the Philippines.
"The Philippine Journey," an interactive exhibit that traces the country's history from pre-colonial times to the present day.
"The Art of Our Time," a collection of contemporary Filipino art that highlights the works of both established and emerging artists.

The Ayala Museum also hosts various events, such as lectures, workshops, and film screenings, to engage the public and foster a greater appreciation for Philippine culture and history.
The museum is housed in a modern, glass-and-steel building designed by architect Carlos Arnaiz. Its location in the heart of Makati City's central business district makes it easily accessible to both locals and tourists.
