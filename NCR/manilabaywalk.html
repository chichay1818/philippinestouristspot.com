Metro Manila

Metro Manila, the National Capital Region of the Philippines, is a bustling metropolis that serves as the country's political, economic, and cultural center. It is composed of 16 cities and one municipality, which together form a highly urbanized and dynamic region.

The area was established in 1975 to consolidate the then-independent cities of Manila, Quezon City, Caloocan, Las Piñas, Makati, Malabon, Mandaluyong, Marikina, Muntinlua, Navotas, Parañaque, Pasay, Pasig, San Juan, Taguig, and Valenzuela, along with Pateros municipality. This move aimed to streamline the administration and development of the region, which is crucial for the nation's progress.



Manila Baywalk

The Manila Baywalk is a scenic promenade situated along the shores of Manila Bay in Manila, Philippines. It stretches along Roxas Boulevard, offering a picturesque waterfront setting for leisurely strolls, recreation, and social gatherings. The Baywalk underwent various phases of development over the years to enhance its facilities and amenities for locals and tourists alike. It features landscaped gardens, palm trees, benches, and lighting installations, creating a pleasant environment for visitors to enjoy the stunning sunset views over Manila Bay.
