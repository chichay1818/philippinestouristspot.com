Manila Zoo

The Manila Zoo, officially known as the Manila Zoological and Botanical Garden, is a zoological park located in Malate, Manila, Philippines. Established in 1959, it is one of the oldest zoos in Asia and serves as a popular recreational and educational destination for locals and tourists. The zoo spans over 5 hectares and is home to a diverse collection of animal species, including mammals, birds, reptiles, and amphibians.

The Manila Zoo aims to promote wildlife conservation and environmental awareness through its exhibits and educational programs. Visitors can explore various animal habitats, such as the African Savannah, Tropical Rainforest, and Aviary, to learn about different ecosystems and the animals that inhabit them. The zoo also offers guided tours, animal feeding sessions, and educational talks conducted by zookeepers and wildlife experts.

Despite facing challenges over the years, including issues related to animal welfare and infrastructure, the Manila Zoo remains a beloved institution and a cultural landmark in the city. Efforts have been made to improve the zoo's facilities and enhance the well-being of its animal residents. As a recreational and educational facility, the Manila Zoo continues to play a valuable role in promoting wildlife conservation and fostering appreciation for the natural world among visitors of all ages.

Sofitel Philippine Plaza Manila is an iconic 5-star luxury resort nestled in an expansive tropical setting. Renowned for its magnificent contrast of captivating sunsets and the sparkling waters of Manila Bay, the hotel attracts international and local travelers alike with its signature Filipino brand of hospitality and the French joie de vivre reflected in its service rituals; from entertainment, food and wine offerings. Nestled adjacent to the historic Cultural Center of the Philippines, Sofitel Philippine Plaza boasts of a location within the city beat and the refreshing tranquillity of the bay.
