Marikina Shoe Museum

The Marikina Shoe Museum is a notable cultural institution located in Marikina City, Philippines. It showcases the rich history and craftsmanship of the local shoe industry, which has been a significant part of the city's heritage for centuries. The museum is housed in a historic building that once served as the city's former Concepcion Mansion. It features a diverse collection of shoes, ranging from traditional indigenous footwear to modern designer creations.

Established in 2001, the Marikina Shoe Museum aims to preserve and promote the artistry and craftsmanship of Marikina's shoemakers. The exhibits highlight the evolution of shoe design and production techniques over the years, offering insights into the cultural and economic significance of the industry to the city and the country as a whole. Visitors can explore various galleries dedicated to different themes, including historical footwear, celebrity shoes, and innovative shoe designs.

Beyond its role as a museum, the Marikina Shoe Museum also serves as a platform for educational programs and cultural events aimed at raising awareness about the local shoe industry and its importance in Marikina's identity. Through its exhibits and activities, the museum celebrates the heritage of shoemaking while also inspiring creativity and innovation in the field.
