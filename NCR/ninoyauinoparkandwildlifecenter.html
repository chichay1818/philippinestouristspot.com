Ninoy Aquino Parks and Wildlife Center

The Ninoy Aquino Parks and Wildlife Center, located in Quezon City, Metro Manila, Philippines, is a sprawling recreational and conservation area named in honor of Senator Benigno "Ninoy" Aquino Jr. The park spans approximately 22.7 hectares and serves as a sanctuary for various flora and fauna species, making it a haven for nature lovers and wildlife enthusiasts.

Established in 1954, the Ninoy Aquino Parks and Wildlife Center features lush greenery, tranquil ponds, and walking trails, providing visitors with opportunities for leisurely strolls and picnics amidst nature. The park is home to a diverse array of plant species, including trees, shrubs, and ornamental plants, as well as a wide variety of birds, fishes, and other wildlife.

In addition to its recreational offerings, the Ninoy Aquino Parks and Wildlife Center also plays a vital role in conservation and environmental education. The park houses a wildlife rescue and rehabilitation center, which cares for injured and confiscated animals, as well as a botanical garden and a mini zoo that showcase native and exotic species. Through its educational programs, guided tours, and interactive exhibits, the park aims to raise awareness about biodiversity conservation and the importance of preserving natural habitats. As a green oasis in the heart of the city, the Ninoy Aquino Parks and Wildlife Center provides a peaceful retreat where visitors can reconnect with nature and appreciate the beauty of the Philippine ecosystem.
