Metro Manila

Metro Manila, the National Capital Region of the Philippines, is a bustling metropolis that serves as the country's political, economic, and cultural center. It is composed of 16 cities and one municipality, which together form a highly urbanized and dynamic region.

The area was established in 1975 to consolidate the then-independent cities of Manila, Quezon City, Caloocan, Las Piñas, Makati, Malabon, Mandaluyong, Marikina, Muntinlua, Navotas, Parañaque, Pasay, Pasig, San Juan, Taguig, and Valenzuela, along with Pateros municipality. This move aimed to streamline the administration and development of the region, which is crucial for the nation's progress.



San Agustin Church

The San Agustin Church is another notable landmark located within Intramuros, the historic walled city of Manila, Philippines. Designated as a UNESCO World Heritage Site, it is one of the oldest stone churches in the Philippines and is renowned for its architectural and historical significance. Constructed in 1587 by Spanish Augustinian friars, the San Agustin Church has survived numerous natural disasters and wars throughout its history. Its architecture is a remarkable example of the Spanish colonial Baroque style, characterized by ornate decorations, intricate details, and grandiose proportions.

The interior of the church is equally impressive, featuring beautifully carved wooden altars, religious statues, and exquisite paintings. The church's convent, which now houses a museum, contains a vast collection of religious artifacts, artwork, and historical documents that provide insight into the country's colonial past. Aside from its architectural and artistic value, the San Agustin Church holds immense religious significance for Filipinos. It continues to serve as an active place of worship and is a popular venue for weddings, religious ceremonies, and cultural events.

Visitors to the San Agustin Church can explore its stunning interior, marvel at its architectural splendor, and learn about its rich history through guided tours and exhibits. It remains one of the must-visit attractions in Manila, offering a glimpse into the country's colonial heritage and religious traditions.



Hotel Nearby

The Manila hotel

The Manila Hotel is a historic luxury hotel located along Roxas Boulevard in Manila, Philippines. It was built in 1912 and has been a symbol of Filipino-American friendship since its inception. The hotel has played host to numerous dignitaries, celebrities, and world leaders throughout its history.

Features:
•The hotel has a variety of room types, from standard rooms to luxurious suites, all designed with comfort and elegance in mind.
•With several restaurants and bars, guests can enjoy a diverse range of cuisines, from Filipino and international dishes to fine dining experiences.
•he hotel boasts an outdoor swimming pool, a fitness center, and a spa for relaxation and rejuvenation.
•The Manila Hotel has numerous function rooms and ballrooms, making it an ideal venue for weddings, conferences, and other special events.


The Bayleaf

The Bayleaf Intramuros Manila is a boutique hotel located within the historic walls of Intramuros, a significant district in Manila, Philippines. Intramuros, meaning "within the walls" in Spanish, is a well-preserved colonial-era walled city that dates back to the Spanish colonial period in the Philippines.The Bayleaf Hotel offers a blend of modern amenities and historic charm, making it a popular choice for both leisure and business travelers. The hotel features well-appointed rooms with views of either the historic city or Manila Bay.

Additionally, it offers various dining options, including a rooftop restaurant where guests can enjoy panoramic views of the city skyline and Manila Bay while indulging in local and international cuisine. Beyond its accommodations and dining, The Bayleaf Intramuros Manila is conveniently located near many of Manila's attractions, including Fort Santiago, San Agustin Church, and Casa Manila. Its central location within Intramuros also provides easy access to other parts of Manila, making it an ideal base for exploring the city's rich history and cultural heritage.
