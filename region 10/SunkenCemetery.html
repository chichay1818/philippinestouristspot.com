Tourist Spot in Region 10

Sunken Cemetery

Sunken Cemetery, nestled along the shores of the volcanic island of Camiguin in the Philippines, is a poignant testament to the island's tumultuous past and resilient spirit. This unique landmark emerged from the catastrophic eruption of Mount Vulcan in the 1870s, which caused a significant portion of the cemetery to sink beneath the waves. Today, the submerged cemetery serves as a solemn reminder of the destructive forces of nature and the lives lost during the volcanic eruption.

One of the most striking features of the Sunken Cemetery is the large cross marker that rises majestically above the water, standing as a solemn sentinel over the sunken graves below. This iconic symbol of faith and remembrance is both haunting and beautiful, attracting visitors from far and wide who come to pay their respects and contemplate the fragility of life. The cross, partially submerged in the tranquil sea, serves as a poignant backdrop for contemplation and reflection, offering solace to those who visit.
