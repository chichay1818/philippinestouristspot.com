Tourist Spot in Region 2

Crocodile Island

Crocodile Island in Cagayan Province, Philippines, is a remarkable natural formation situated off the coast of the town of Gonzaga. This uninhabited islet is named after its distinct shape, resembling that of a crocodile, and is renowned for its rugged cliffs, pristine beaches, and rich biodiversity. Accessible by boat from the mainland, Crocodile Island offers visitors a chance to immerse themselves in the beauty of untouched nature and explore its unique landscape.

The island's rocky shores and crystal-clear waters make it an ideal destination for snorkeling and diving enthusiasts. Beneath the surface, vibrant coral reefs abound, providing shelter to a diverse array of marine life, including colorful fish, sea turtles, and various species of invertebrates. Snorkelers can glide through the water, observing the mesmerizing underwater world and encountering fascinating creatures in their natural habitat.

In addition to its underwater wonders, Crocodile Island offers breathtaking panoramic views of the surrounding seascape. Visitors can hike to elevated vantage points on the island to take in sweeping vistas of the ocean, nearby islets, and distant horizons. With its rugged charm, ecological significance, and opportunities for outdoor adventure, Crocodile Island stands as a testament to the natural beauty and biodiversity of Cagayan Province
