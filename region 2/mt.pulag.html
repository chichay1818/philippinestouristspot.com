Tourist Spot in Region 2

Mt.Pulag National Park

Mt. Pulag National Park is a majestic natural wonder located in the Cordillera Central mountain range on the island of Luzon in the Philippines. Spanning across the provinces of Benguet, Ifugao, and Nueva Vizcaya, it boasts an elevation of 2,926 meters above sea level, making it the third highest peak in the Philippines. The park is renowned for its breathtaking landscapes, diverse ecosystems, and rich biodiversity, drawing adventurers, nature enthusiasts, and researchers alike.

One of the most iconic features of Mt. Pulag National Park is its renowned sea of clouds phenomenon, where low-lying clouds blanket the surrounding valleys, creating a surreal and ethereal vista that captivates visitors. This mesmerizing sight is best experienced during the early hours of dawn from the summit, offering a truly magical experience that is unmatched elsewhere in the country.

Beyond its scenic beauty, Mt. Pulag National Park is also a haven for biodiversity, harboring a wide array of flora and fauna, including rare and endemic species. The park is home to various habitats, including mossy forests, grasslands, and dwarf bamboo groves, each supporting unique plant and animal life. Visitors can encounter a plethora of wildlife, from endemic bird species to elusive mammals such as the Philippine deer and cloud rats. With its stunning landscapes and rich biodiversity, Mt. Pulag National Park stands as a testament to the natural splendor of the Philippines and is a must-visit destination for anyone seeking adventure and immersion in nature.
