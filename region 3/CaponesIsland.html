Capones Island

Capones Island, located off the coast of Brgy. Pundaquit in San Antonio, Zambales, is a hidden gem in the Philippines known for its pristine beaches, crystal-clear waters, and stunning rock formations. Accessible by boat, this picturesque island offers a peaceful retreat for those looking to escape the hustle and bustle of city life.

One of the main attractions of Capones Island is its historic Spanish-era lighthouse, which stands majestically atop a hill overlooking the sea. Built in 1890, the lighthouse served as a beacon for ships navigating the waters of the West Philippine Sea. Today, it stands as a silent witness to the island's rich history and offers visitors a breathtaking panoramic view of the surrounding area.

In addition to its natural beauty, Capones Island also offers a glimpse into the region's history and culture. The island is believed to have been named after Captain Jose Capones, a Spanish soldier who was stationed in the area during the colonial period. Today, visitors can still see remnants of the island's colonial past, including old Spanish-era structures and artifacts.
