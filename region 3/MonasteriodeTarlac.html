Tourist Spots in Region 3




Monasterio de Tarlac

The Monastery of Tarlac, located in the province of Tarlac, Philippines, is a sacred site perched atop Mount Resurrection. Its towering statue of Jesus Christ, standing at 30 meters tall, makes it a notable landmark in Asia. Pilgrims and tourists are drawn to its tranquil atmosphere and panoramic views of the surrounding landscape, offering a serene backdrop for prayer and reflection.


For Catholics in the Philippines, the monastery holds significant religious importance, serving as a place of pilgrimage and spiritual renewal. Devotees flock to its chapel dedicated to the Risen Christ for prayer vigils, mass celebrations, and stations of the cross. The monastery's peaceful ambiance fosters a sense of reverence and contemplation, inviting visitors to connect with their faith amidst the natural beauty of the region.


In addition to its religious significance, the Monastery of Tarlac is committed to environmental stewardship and eco-tourism. Nestled amidst lush greenery, it promotes conservation efforts through hiking trails, botanical gardens, and educational programs. Visitors can engage in activities that raise awareness about environmental issues while enjoying the scenic beauty of the monastery's surroundings. With its blend of spirituality and environmental consciousness, the Monastery of Tarlac continues to inspire and uplift all who visit.
