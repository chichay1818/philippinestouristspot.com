San Fernando

San Fernando, located in the Philippines, is a city with a rich heritage that showcases its history and culture. During the National Heritage Month, the city government of San Fernando organizes a heritage open house and free Kalesa tour, allowing visitors to explore and learn more about the town's history and culture. This event features historic items and provides a glimpse into the city's past source.


At the heart of downtown San Fernando is the historic poblacion, which is filled with remnants of the city's architectural heritage and history. The city's urban renewal program, called Preserving Heritage for Progress, focuses on preserving these architectural legacies and intangible cultural aspects of the city. This initiative aims to protect and showcase San Fernando's rich heritage for future generations to appreciate and enjoy source.


San Fernando has a deep historical significance. The city is named after Fernando VI and is placed under the patronage of San Fernando III, King of Castile and Leon in Spain. The town of San Fernando was established in 1754 and has a long history dating back to the Spanish colonial period. The first church in San Fernando was built in 1755, featuring wooden walls and nipa roofing, reflecting the architectural style of that era source.
