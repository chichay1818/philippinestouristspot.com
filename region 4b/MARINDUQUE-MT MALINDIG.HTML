MARINDUQUE


Marinduque is a heart-shaped island which lies between the Bondoc Peninsula at the south- eastern part of Luzon and the island of Mindoro. It is bounded by four bodies of water, on the north by Tayabas Bay, northeast by Mongpong Pass, South-east by Tayabas Strait, and Sibuyan at the south. The first inhabitants of Marinduque were Malay natives. They established trade relation with the Chinese as early as the Sung Dynasty as evidenced by the artifacts and shards still found in caves that abound on the island.



MOUNT MALINDIG

Known to be the highest peak/vantage point in Marinduque, Mount Malindig is an active stratovolcano. This mountain also has names such as Mount Marlanga and Mount Malindik. Because of its location, at the peak you will be able to see panoramic views of Verde Island, Burias Island, Mindoro and more.


Mount Malindig is popular for locals and tourists as it is also known for its biodiversity such as its wild orchids. There are three peaks on this mountain and occasionally you'll see cattle as this area is popular for cattle grazing.


Mount Malindig is a large potentially active stratovolcano on the island of Marinduque in the Philippines. It is the highest peak in the island having an elevation of 1,157 metres above sea level. The name Marinduque itself may have been derived from Marindik or Malindik.


Nearby Hotels


MALBOG RESORT- Malbog Sulfuric Hot Spring Resort or “Mabaho” is located at the foot of the mountain in Brgy. Malbog, Buenavista, Marinduque. The hot spring has sulfur content popularly used for skin care and treatments. Despite its rotten-egg like foul, many locals and tourists visit this place for body relaxation and healing of skin conditions. Some are even bringing home bottles of sulfuric water.
