Tubbataha Reefs Natural Park

A UNESCO World Heritage Site and one of the best dive sites in the world, Tubbataha Reef is home to a rich diversity of marine species and stunning coral formations. Tubbataha is home to a wide array of marine life, including over 600 species of fish, 360 species of corals, 11 species of sharks, 13 species of dolphins and whales, and various seabirds and turtles. Tubbataha Reefs Natural Park is a protected area, managed by the Tubbataha Management Office (TMO), to ensure the preservation of its marine resources. Strict regulations are in place to minimize human impact on the ecosystem.
