Tourist Spots in Region 4B

Mimaropa (officially stylized in all caps), officially the Southwestern Tagalog Region[ (Filipino: Rehiyong Timog-Kanlurang Tagalog), is an administrative region in the Philippines. It is one of two regions in the country having no land border with another region (the other being Eastern Visayas). The name is an acronym combination of its constituent provinces: Mindoro (divided into Occidental Mindoro and Oriental Mindoro), Marinduque, Romblon and Palawan.

Mt. Halcon

The mountain is renowned among hikers and mountaineers for its challenging trails and rugged terrain. Climbing Mount Halcon requires careful planning, physical endurance, and proper equipment due to its steep slopes, dense forests, and unpredictable weather conditions. The climb typically takes several days to complete, with camping required along the way.


Despite its difficulty, Mount Halcon offers stunning views of the surrounding landscapes and provides adventurers with an opportunity to experience the natural beauty and biodiversity of Oriental Mindoro. It is also home to a variety of endemic plant and animal species, including the critically endangered tamaraw (Bubalus mindorensis), a type of dwarf buffalo found only in Mindoro.


Due to its ecological significance and challenging trekking routes, climbing Mount Halcon is regulated by local authorities to ensure the safety of climbers and the preservation of the mountain's fragile ecosystem. Those interested in climbing Mount Halcon are advised to coordinate with local mountaineering groups, obtain necessary permits, and adhere to responsible trekking practices.
