Tourist Spots in Region 4B

Mimaropa (officially stylized in all caps), officially the Southwestern Tagalog Region[ (Filipino: Rehiyong Timog-Kanlurang Tagalog), is an administrative region in the Philippines. It is one of two regions in the country having no land border with another region (the other being Eastern Visayas). The name is an acronym combination of its constituent provinces: Mindoro (divided into Occidental Mindoro and Oriental Mindoro), Marinduque, Romblon and Palawan.



Lubang Island

Lubang Island is a picturesque island in the South China Sea, known for its rugged terrain, pristine beaches, and rich marine biodiversity. It is the largest island in Occidental Mindoro and offers a range of activities for visitors, including snorkeling, diving, beachcombing, and hiking. With its stunning landscapes and tranquil atmosphere, Lubang Island is a popular destination for tourists seeking a tropical getaway in the Philippines.

Lubang Island is becoming increasingly popular as a tourist destination, known for its natural beauty, clear waters, and diverse marine life. Visitors can enjoy activities such as snorkeling, diving, beachcombing, and island hopping. Some of the notable attractions on the island include Agkawayan Beach, Tilik Port, and Cabra Island.

Lubang Island has a rich history dating back to pre-colonial times when it was inhabited by indigenous peoples. It gained historical significance during World War II as the hiding place of Japanese soldier Hiroo Onoda, who continued to fight a guerrilla war for decades after the war ended, unaware that it had concluded.

hotel and accommodation in Lubang Island

Lubang Beach Resort: This beach resort offers basic accommodations and is one of the few lodging options available on the island. It's located in Barangay Looc and provides beachfront cottages and rooms.Beach Activities: Enjoy swimming, sunbathing, beachcombing, and picnicking on the island's pristine beaches. Popular beaches include Looc Beach, Tagbac Beach, and Tilik Beach. Snorkeling and Diving: Explore the underwater world around Lubang Island with snorkeling or diving trips. The island's clear waters offer opportunities to see colorful coral reefs and diverse marine life. Island Hopping: Take a boat tour around Lubang Island and visit nearby smaller islands such as Cabra Island. Enjoy snorkeling, swimming, and relaxing on secluded beaches. Trekking and Hiking: Embark on hiking adventures to explore Lubang Island's rugged terrain and rolling hills. Mount Banoi is the highest peak on the island and offers panoramic views of the surrounding area. Cultural Immersion: Engage with the local community and learn about their traditional way of life, culture, and customs. Visit local villages, interact with residents, and sample traditional Filipino dishes. Birdwatching: Lubang Island is home to various bird species, making it a great destination for birdwatching enthusiasts. Keep an eye out for endemic and migratory birds while exploring the island's forests and coastal areas.


Homestays: Some locals in Lubang Island may offer homestay accommodations for tourists. This can be a great way to experience the local culture and hospitality firsthand.


tourist spot in Lubang Island

Looc Beach: Looc Beach is one of the most popular beaches on Lubang Island, known for its fine white sand and clear turquoise waters. It's an excellent spot for swimming, sunbathing, and picnicking.
