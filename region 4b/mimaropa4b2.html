Apo Reef Natural Park

Apo Reef is a natural marine park located in the Philippines, specifically in the western waters of Occidental Mindoro province. It is considered one of the largest contiguous reef systems in the country and is a UNESCO World Heritage Site. Apo Reef is situated in the Sulu Sea, approximately 15 nautical miles west of Sablayan town in Occidental Mindoro. It is part of the municipality of Sablayan and is within the jurisdiction of the Province of Occidental Mindoro. Apo Reef is the largest contiguous coral reef system in the Philippines. It covers an area of about 34 square kilometers and is composed of two coral reefs, namely Apo Island and Apo Menor.


The reef is known for its rich marine biodiversity, with over 500 species of marine life recorded, including various species of fish, corals, mollusks, and other invertebrates. It is also a habitat for endangered species such as sea turtles and sharks. Apo Reef was declared a Natural Park and Protected Area in 1996 under Republic Act No. 7586, also known as the National Integrated Protected Areas System (NIPAS) Act of 1992. The park is managed and protected by the Department of Environment and Natural Resources (DENR)


Apo Reef Natural Park is a popular destination for snorkeling, diving, and beach camping. Visitors can explore the vibrant underwater ecosystems, swim alongside colorful fish and corals, and relax on the pristine white sand beaches. Guided tours are available, and visitors are required to coordinate with accredited tour operators for their visit. Apo Reef Natural Park focus on protecting its marine biodiversity, preventing illegal fishing activities, and promoting sustainable tourism practices. The park management works closely with local communities, government agencies, and non-governmental organizations to ensure the long-term preservation of the reef ecosystem.


Nearby Hotels and Accomodation

When planning a visit to Apo Reef, you'll need to arrange for accommodation in Sablayan. There are several resorts, guesthouses, and homestays in Sablayan that cater to tourists visiting the area. Some popular options include:

Pandan Island Resort - Located on Pandan Island, this resort offers cottages and beachfront accommodations. It's a popular choice for tourists exploring Apo Reef due to its proximity to Sablayan.


Sablayan Adventure Camp - This camp offers a range of accommodations, from dormitories to private cottages. They also organize tours to Apo Reef and other attractions in the area.


Before planning your trip, it's advisable to check the latest information on accommodations and tour operators in Sablayan, as options may change over time. Additionally, make sure to arrange your trip to Apo Reef with a reputable tour operator who can provide transportation and guides for your visit to the natural park.
