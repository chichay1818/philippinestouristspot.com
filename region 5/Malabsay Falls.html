Tourist Spots in Region 5


Malabsay Falls

Malabsay Falls, a hidden gem tucked away within the lush forests near Mount Isarog in Camarines Sur, Philippines, offers a serene oasis for nature lovers and adventure seekers alike. Fed by the pristine waters flowing from the mountain's slopes, this captivating waterfall cascades from a height of approximately 40 feet into a crystal-clear pool below, surrounded by verdant foliage and towering trees. The falls derive their name from the local Bicolano term "malabsay," which means clear or transparent, aptly describing the clarity of the water as it plunges gracefully over the rocky cliffs.


Visiting Malabsay Falls is not only an opportunity to marvel at its natural beauty but also a chance to partake in various outdoor activities amidst the stunning scenery. Adventurous souls can embark on a trek through the verdant forest trails leading to the falls, immersing themselves in the sights and sounds of nature along the way. The hike offers glimpses of diverse flora and fauna endemic to the region, adding to the allure of the journey. Upon reaching the falls, visitors can take a refreshing dip in the cool, emerald waters of the natural pool, surrounded by the soothing sounds of cascading water and the tranquility of the forest.


Malabsay Falls serves as a peaceful retreat from the hustle and bustle of urban life, inviting visitors to unwind and reconnect with nature in its purest form. Whether seeking adventure, relaxation, or simply a moment of solitude in a pristine natural setting, a visit to Malabsay Falls promises an unforgettable experience amidst the beauty of Mount Isarog's foothills.
