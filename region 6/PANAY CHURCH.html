Tourist Spots in Region 6




Pan-ay Church

Pan-ay Church officially known as the Santa Monica Parish Church, is a historical and architectural landmark located in Panay, Capiz, Philippines. Built during the Spanish colonial period in the 18th century, the church is renowned for its impressive baroque-style architecture and rich cultural heritage.


The church's facade features intricate details and ornate carvings, showcasing the craftsmanship of Filipino artisans from centuries past. Its massive bell tower stands as a testament to the church's significance, housing the famous Pan-ay Bell, which is considered one of the largest bells in Asia.


Visitors can admire the beautifully preserved interior adorned with religious artworks, wooden altars, and ceiling paintings depicting scenes from the Bible. The church holds religious significance for the local community and serves as a place of worship and pilgrimage for Catholics..
