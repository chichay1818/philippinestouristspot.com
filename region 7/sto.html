Sto. Nino Shrine and Heritage Museum

Sto. Nino Shrine and Heritage Museum, Real Street, Tacloban, Leyte Province Philippines.Art Collections gifted to the Marcos family during the time of the late Pres. Ferdinand Marcos, Sr.The park was built to commemorate the historic landing of Gen. Douglas MacArthur in Leyte Gulf in his campaign to liberate the Philippines from Japanese occupation. This event led to the largest naval battle of World War II.The park is located in the Municipality of Palo and is one of the most visited sites in Leyte. Main feature is the larger than life bronze statue of Gen Douglas MacArthur.

The museum features a chapel with the image of Sto. Ni√±o as its focal point, along with 13 guest rooms adorned with motifs representing different regions of the Philippines. Visitors can explore the spacious ballroom and the former bed chambers of the Marcos family, gaining insights into the opulent lifestyle they once led.Throughout the museum, guests can admire a variety of historical paintings, antique ceramics, pottery, and collectibles from different countries, including chandeliers from the Czech Republic, mirrors from Austria, tiles from Italy, and more.The museum also houses a collection of Imelda Marcos' personal belongings and memorabilia, providing a unique perspective on her life and influence during the Marcos regime.
