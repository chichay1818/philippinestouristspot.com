Tourist Spot in Region 9

Fort Pilar

Situated in Zamboanga City, Fort Pilar is a 17th-century military defense fortress built by the Spanish colonial government. Today, it serves as a historical shrine and museum showcasing relics, artifacts, and exhibits related to Zamboanga's history and culture.It was originally called the Real Fuerte de San José (Royal Fort of Saint Joseph) when construction began in 1635, led by Jesuit priest-engineer Father Melchor de Vera.

The fort was later renamed the Real Fuerte de Nuestra Señora del Pilar de Zaragoza (Royal Fort of Our Lady of the Pillar of Saragossa), in reference to a venerated image of the Virgin Mary.According to local tradition, the Virgin Mary is said to have appeared at the fort on multiple occasions, including in 1734 and 1897 to protect the city.
